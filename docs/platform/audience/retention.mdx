---
title: Retention
description: How audience retention is measured — returning users, cohort curves, the DAU/MAU stickiness ratio, and activity-based vs. feature-based retention.
---

**Retention** measures how reliably an audience comes back. The Audience section reports retention as a complement to acquisition: a site with strong acquisition but weak retention is renting traffic, not building one.

### Returning vs. new users

The simplest retention signal is the share of returning users in the current window. Each session carries `$is_first_session` from PostHog; the dashboard inverts that flag to produce the returning-user count.

| Cohort | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| New users | First-ever session falls inside the selected window |
| Returning users | First-ever session predates the selected window |

A healthy mix depends on the site's lifecycle stage. Early-stage sites typically show 70%+ new; mature sites stabilize around 30–50% new with the rest returning.

### Cohort retention curves

The dashboard groups users by their **week of first visit** (the acquisition cohort) and tracks what share return in each subsequent week. This produces the standard retention curve.

| Day after first visit | Typical content-publisher retention |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------|
| Day 1 | 20–35% |
| Day 7 | 8–15% |
| Day 30 | 3–8% |

These benchmarks vary heavily by content vertical. News and reference sites often see flatter, more reliable retention. Long-form publications see steep early drop-off but a stable long tail of monthly returnees.

### Activity-based vs. feature-based retention

Two retention questions deserve separate measurement:

- **Activity-based retention** — did the user come back at all? Any tracked event in the cohort window counts. This is the default the dashboard reports.
- **Feature-based retention** — did the user come back **and use a specific feature** (search, account, paid content, newsletter signup)? This filters the retention numerator to events of a particular type.

Activity retention shows whether the brand is sticky. Feature retention shows whether a specific surface is sticky. Both matter, but they answer different questions.

### Stickiness (DAU/MAU)

The DAU/MAU ratio is a single-number summary of how habit-forming the product is. See [`./dau`](./dau) and [`./mau`](./mau) for the full definitions; the ratio is computed as the average [DAU](./dau) over the window divided by [MAU](./mau) at window close.

| Stickiness | Interpretation |
|------------|----------------|
| > 0.50 | Daily-habit product (messaging, social) |
| 0.20 – 0.50 | Strong engagement (productivity, news) |
| 0.10 – 0.20 | Moderate (most content publishers) |
| < 0.10 | Episodic — users visit on specific triggers only |

A rising stickiness ratio at flat MAU is the cleanest signal that retention is improving — the existing audience is showing up more often, not just being topped up by acquisition.
