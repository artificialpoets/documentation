The **Active Users** page is the simplest possible answer to "how many people are using the product." It counts distinct users who took at least one meaningful action — a pageview, a click, a scroll — inside a configurable time window.

The page sits at the top of the Audience section because every other audience metric is a slice or a derivative of this one.

### What "active" means

A user is **active** on day D if they took at least one tracked action in the 24-hour window ending at the end of day D. The Platform counts users by their stable identity (a hashed cookie / device id), deduplicated across pages and sessions inside the window.

A user who came back five times in one day counts as one active user that day. Two users on the same device — different cookies, same machine — count as two.

### Three default windows

The page shows three rolling counts side by side:

| Window | What it is | Read it as |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **DAU** | [Daily active users](./dau) — active in the last 24 hours | Today's reach |
| **WAU** | Weekly active users — active in the last 7 days | This week's reach |
| **MAU** | [Monthly active users](./mau) — active in the last 30 days | This month's reach |

Each is reported with a sparkline showing the last 90 days, and a delta vs. the previous equal-length window.

### The stickiness ratio

Below the three counts you'll see the **DAU/MAU ratio** — a single number between 0 and 1. It tells you what fraction of your monthly users are active on any given day.

| DAU/MAU | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| > 0.5 | Daily-use product (rare for news/content sites) |
| 0.2 – 0.5 | Weekly-use product (typical for newsletter-driven sites) |
| < 0.2 | Episodic-use (typical for news event-driven traffic) |

A trend in this ratio is more informative than the raw counts. A growing audience with falling stickiness usually means new visitors are arriving but not coming back.

### Filtering and segmentation

The page supports the standard Audience filters at the top:

- **Site** — restrict to one site or roll up across all
- **Device** — phone, tablet, desktop
- **Channel** — see [Traffic Channel](./traffic-channel) for the taxonomy
- **Real-human bucket** — by default only `real human` sessions are counted; toggle to see uncertain or all

The counts update live as filters change. Filter combinations that produce too few sessions for stable counts will display a "low-sample" badge.

### What is *not* on this page

- Per-article reach — that's [Content → Top inventory](../content/top-inventory).
- New vs. returning split — that's [Retention](./retention), which slices Active Users by whether they appeared in earlier windows.
- Acquisition channel composition over time — that's [Acquisition](./acquisition).

This page answers the size question. Other pages answer the composition and behavior questions.
