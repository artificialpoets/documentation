The **Audience Intent** page groups your visitors by what they came to do. Every session that includes a search query or a meaningful navigation pattern gets a label from the [Intent classifier](../platform/intent-classifier): informational, navigational, transactional, or investigational.

The page is most useful when you treat intent as a lens. The same audience-size number means very different things depending on whether it's mostly people researching a topic or mostly people about to convert.

### What you see

The page has three stacked panels:

1. **Intent mix** — a stacked bar showing how the four intent labels split your sessions over time. A growing transactional slice usually correlates with a marketing push; a shrinking one is often a leading indicator for revenue.
2. **Per-intent funnels** — for each intent type, the conversion rate from initial query → engaged session → goal completion. Lets you spot intent types that are arriving but not converting.
3. **Top content by intent** — the articles, pages, and audio items most often consumed by each intent label. Useful for spotting which content does what work.

### The four intent labels

The platform's classifier emits one of four mutually exclusive labels per session. The boundaries are tuned for content/media use cases, not e-commerce:

| Label | The user is trying to | Example signal |
|-------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Informational** | Learn about a topic | "how does X work" search; long-scroll on explainer content |
| **Navigational** | Reach a specific page | Brand-name search; direct entry to a known URL |
| **Transactional** | Take an action | "buy", "subscribe", "sign up" queries; click on a CTA |
| **Investigational** | Compare options | "X vs Y", "best", "review" queries |

Each label arrives with a **confidence score**. Sessions where the model's confidence is below the [min-confidence](../revenue/min-confidence) threshold are bucketed as "unknown" rather than mislabelled.

### How intent connects to other sections

Intent is one of the few signals that crosses section boundaries. You'll see it referenced from:

- [Content → Search stats](../content/search-stats) — search queries broken down by intent
- [Content → Content intent](../content/content-intent) — articles labelled by the intent type they best satisfy
- [Revenue → Journey optimization](../revenue/journey-optimization) — conversion paths grouped by entry intent

The Audience version of intent is the **inbound** view: who's arriving, what they want. The Content version is the **content** view: which articles each intent type consumes. The Revenue version is the **funnel** view: how each intent type converts.

### Reading the mix

The most common pattern: a flat informational slice (steady research traffic) with episodic spikes in transactional (campaign-driven) and investigational (product-launch-driven). If your investigational and transactional slices stay near zero, the platform is likely undersampling — probably because your queries are too thin to classify with confidence. The min-confidence threshold may need lowering for your account.

### What is *not* on this page

- Per-session timeline — that's the Revenue journey reports.
- Intent of users *outside* the session window — intent is session-scoped; it does not roll up to user-level personas.
- A manual labelling interface — labels come from the model, not from editorial tagging. If you want editorial intent metadata on articles, set it in your CMS and the platform will read it as a separate field.
