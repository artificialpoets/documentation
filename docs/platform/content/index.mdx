The **Content** section reports on the published catalog: what's getting attention, how content is classified by intent, and how device segment changes the picture. Where Audience tells you who visited, Content tells you what they read.

### What this section covers

| Page | What it covers |
|------|----------------|
| [Top Inventory](./top-inventory) | The "what's working" view — ranking pages by views and engagement |
| [Content Intent](./content-intent) | How pages are classified by user intent (informational, navigational, commercial, transactional) |
| [Search Intent](./search-intent) | The same taxonomy applied to inbound search queries |
| [Device Segment](./device-segment) | Filter that splits data by Desktop, Mobile, or Combined |
| [Bot Pageviews](./bot-pageviews) | The bot-traffic exclusion rules and counts |

### How to read the section

[Top Inventory](./top-inventory) is the most-used view in the section — it's the daily editorial scan. [Content Intent](./content-intent) and [Search Intent](./search-intent) share a taxonomy on purpose, so comparing the two is the cleanest way to find gaps between what the catalog is **for** and what audiences are **asking for**. [Device Segment](./device-segment) and [Bot Pageviews](./bot-pageviews) are filters that affect every other report in the section.
