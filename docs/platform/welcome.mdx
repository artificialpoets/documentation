The Platform documentation is organized around the questions you can ask of your audience and content. Each top-level section maps to a tab in the dashboard, and each page inside it covers either a concept, a metric, or a specific view you'll see in the product.

This page is your map. Skim the section summaries below, then jump to the area that matches what you're trying to learn.

## How the docs are organized

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [Audience](./audience/) | Who's visiting — acquisition channels, active-user counts, returning visitors, retention. |
| [Content](./content/) | What's being read — top pages, content classification by intent, device segmentation, bot traffic. |
| [Engagement](./engagement/) | How people interact — sessions, pageviews, scroll depth, search behavior, bounce rate. |
| [Performance](./performance/) | How fast your site feels — Core Web Vitals (LCP, FCP, INP, CLS), per-page breakdowns, SEO risk. |
| [Revenue](./revenue/) | What converts — user journeys, high-intent content, sponsor opportunities. |
| [Glossary](./glossary) | Alphabetical index of every metric and term used in the dashboard. |

## Where to start

Pick the entry point that matches your role:

- **"I'm new — show me around."** Read the [Audience overview](./audience/) and [Performance overview](./performance/). Together they answer "how many people are here and how does the site feel."
- **"I'm an editor or content strategist."** Start with [Content](./content/) and [Engagement](./engagement/). They surface which articles work and how readers move through them.
- **"I'm responsible for site performance."** Jump to [Performance](./performance/) → [Core Web Vitals](./performance/core-web-vitals) and then [Traffic Loss Risk](./performance/traffic-loss-risk) to see what's costing you ranking.
- **"I'm focused on monetization."** Start in [Revenue](./revenue/) — [Journey Optimization](./revenue/journey-optimization) and [High-Intent Content](./revenue/high-intent-content) are the two pages to bookmark.
- **"I just need to look up what a metric means."** Open the [Glossary](./glossary) and search for the term.

## How to read a metric page

Every metric page in the dashboard has a dedicated docs page. They follow the same shape:

1. **What it is** — a one-paragraph definition.
2. **How it's calculated** — the formula or rule the Platform applies, plus any thresholds (e.g. "good ≤ 2.5 s" for LCP).
3. **How to interpret it** — what high and low values mean, with the relevant caveats.
4. **How to use it in the dashboard** — which filters and breakdowns help you act on the number.

If a page mentions another term in **bold**, the first mention links to that term's definition. Hover the link to see a short summary without leaving the page; click to open the full page.

## A note on numbers

Web analytics is a noisy measurement. The Platform reports values at the **75th percentile** (p75) by default — meaning 75% of measurements fall at or below the displayed number — to surface the typical experience without being skewed by extreme outliers. The [p75](./performance/p75) glossary entry explains the trade-offs and when to look at other percentiles.
