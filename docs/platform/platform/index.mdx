The **Platform** section is the engine room. The rest of the docs cover what the platform produces — audience charts, recommendations, performance reports. This section covers how those outputs are built: where your content enters, which models process it, and how the results reach your readers.

You do not need to read this section to use the platform day-to-day. The dashboard renders the same information through visual surfaces. But when you want to know *why* a recommendation looked the way it did, or what changed when a model was upgraded, this is the section to consult.

### How the platform is organized

Everything in this section maps to one of five lanes. Content arrives on the left, predictions flow out on the right:

1. **[Ingest](./ingest)** — connectors pulling text, audio, and video from your CMS, podcast host, and video platform.
2. **[Inference](./inference)** — AI models running on that content to produce embeddings, transcripts, intent labels, and recommendations.
3. **[Storage](./storage)** — three stores that hold raw content, vectors, and analytical aggregates.
4. **[Serving](./serving)** — APIs and embeds that hand outputs to your application in real time.
5. **[Observability](./observability)** — the platform watching itself, so anomalies surface before you notice them.

The [Instances](./instances) page renders this as a live map. The [Overview](./overview) page summarizes the same flow as four KPIs and a list of connected sources.
