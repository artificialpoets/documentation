The **Depth** page measures how far users get inside a single piece of content. It is the answer to "yes, they clicked — but did they read it?" Pageviews count opens; depth counts engagement *inside* the page.

The metric on this page is [scroll depth](./scroll-depth), reported at percentile thresholds.

### What gets measured

Every page tracks the maximum scroll position the user reached, expressed as a percentage of the document's scrollable height. The Platform also tracks **reading-stage milestones** — discrete checkpoints corresponding to common content structures.

| Milestone | Default trigger | What it indicates |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Started** | 1% scroll, > 5s on page | The user committed past the headline/hero |
| **Engaged** | 25% scroll | The user is past the intro and into the body |
| **Half** | 50% scroll | The user is mid-article |
| **Most** | 75% scroll | The user is into the closing arguments |
| **Finished** | 90% scroll OR `reading-stage="end"` event | The user reached the end |

The thresholds are configurable per site. On long-form content you may want to push "Finished" to 95% or rely on the explicit event; on short articles 90% may already be the end of the body.

### Two views

The page shows depth in two complementary ways:

1. **Distribution histogram** — how depth is spread across sessions on the selected content slice. A spike at 25% with a long tail beyond it is healthy; a wall at 5% means most visitors never engaged.
2. **Milestone funnel** — the conversion rate from one stage to the next. Lets you spot where readers consistently drop off (e.g., 90% start, 60% engage, 25% half — meaning the intro is fine but the body is too long).

### Depth vs. dwell time

Depth and dwell time are correlated but not interchangeable.

| Signal | What it captures | Easy to game |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Depth | Spatial progress through the document | Hard — requires actual scroll input |
| Dwell time | Wall-clock time on the page | Easy — open and walk away |

Both are needed. A page with high depth and low dwell is being scanned, not read. A page with high dwell and low depth is loaded as a tab and left alone.

### Caveats

A few situations distort depth in ways worth knowing:

- **Infinite scroll** — depth percentages are computed against the document's full height. When the document grows as the user scrolls, the percentage moves down rather than up. See [Infinite scroll](./infinite-scroll) for the right way to read those sessions.
- **Mobile vs. desktop** — at the same content length, mobile users typically reach higher depth percentages because the same body height = more scrolling. Filter by device when comparing.
- **Image-heavy pages** — depth is computed pixel-wise. A heavily illustrated page may show low average depth not because users disengaged but because the body of the page is *tall*.

### What is *not* on this page

- Per-paragraph attention — depth is page-level, not selection-level.
- Heatmaps — pointer/click heatmaps live in the [Sites](../sites) detail view, not here.
- Reading speed inferences — the Platform does not estimate reading time from depth + dwell; reading is too variable per user.
