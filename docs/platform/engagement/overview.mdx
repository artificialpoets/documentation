title: Engagement Overview
description: A tour of the Engagement section — what each metric reports, what question it answers, and where to drill in for deeper analysis.
---

The **Engagement** section reports how users behave once they arrive on the site. Audience tells you how many people visited; Engagement tells you what they did. The section is organized around three questions: how much did users read, how deep did they go, and what did they look for?

### How much did users read?

The volume metrics establish the baseline.

| Metric | Question it answers |
|--------|---------------------|
| [Pageview](./pageview) | How many individual page loads happened? |
| [Total pageviews](./total-pageviews) | Aggregated pageview volume across the window |
| [Session](./session) | How is a single visit defined and bounded? |
| [Pageviews per session](./pageviews-per-session) | On average, how many pages did a visit cover? |
| [Average session duration](./average-session-duration) | How long did the typical visit last? |

Volume metrics are best read together — pageviews alone can grow because of more visitors, more pages per visit, or more sessions. The breakdown matters.

### How deep did users go?

Depth metrics show whether visitors actually engaged with content or bounced off.

| Metric | Question it answers |
|--------|---------------------|
| [Bounce rate](./bounce-rate) | What share of sessions left after one page? |
| [Scroll depth](./scroll-depth) | How far down each page did users scroll? |
| [Reading stage](./reading-stage) | How does scroll depth bucket into reading-completion stages? |
| [Infinite scroll rate](./infinite-scroll-rate) | What share of pageviews came from infinite-scroll loads? |

Depth is where editorial work shows up. A page with high pageviews but stage-1 scroll depth is leaking attention right at the fold. See [`./infinite-scroll`](./infinite-scroll) for how the infinite-scroll pattern interacts with these numbers.

### What did users look for?

Search analytics surface the queries users typed and how the site responded.

| Metric | Question it answers |
|--------|---------------------|
| [CTR](./ctr) | What share of searches led to a click? |
| [Search funnel conversion rate](./search-funnel-conversion-rate) | What share of users who searched then engaged? |
| [Empty-result searches](./empty-result-searches) | What queries returned nothing — and how often? |

See [`./search`](./search) for the section-level walkthrough of how search analytics are captured and what each report reveals.

### How to use this section

Start with the depth metrics whenever a page or section shows traffic that isn't converting into expected outcomes. Volume metrics are useful for tracking trends; depth and search metrics are where actionable editorial decisions originate.
