The **Revenue** section connects the engagement data the rest of the Platform produces with the question every publisher eventually asks: which content, audiences, and journeys actually make money? Revenue is not a billing or commerce dashboard. It is a set of analyses that surface the signals correlated with monetization potential, so editorial and product teams can act on them before the conversion happens.

### What the section covers

Three workflows make up the Revenue section, each with its own dedicated page.

| Workflow | What it answers | Where to start |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Journey optimization | Where in a typical user journey does revenue leak out? | [`./journey-optimization`](./journey-optimization) |
| High-intent content | Which pages attract users who are ready to convert? | [`./high-intent-content`](./high-intent-content) |
| Sponsor discovery | Which brands match the site's audience and content profile? | [`./sponsor-discovery`](./sponsor-discovery) |

The three workflows share data — content intent feeds into all of them, journey steps feed into the first two — but ask distinct questions and produce distinct outputs.

### Journey optimization

Models each user's session as a sequence of steps and surfaces where revenue intent drops off. Most monetized sites lose more revenue to drop-off between steps in the journey than to any single content or design problem. The journey reports turn those drop-offs into specific, addressable steps.

### High-intent content

Combines [`../content/search-intent`](../content/search-intent) classifications with engagement signals — low bounce rate, high session duration, deep scroll — to surface pages most likely to convert. This is the editorial-investment view: where should refresh and expansion effort go to maximize conversion lift?

### Sponsor discovery

Builds an audience-and-content profile of the site and matches it against potential sponsors. For publishers running direct sponsorship deals (newsletter sponsorships, branded content, integrated campaigns), this is where the prospect list originates.

### How to read the section

The three workflows are designed to be read in any order. A team focused on conversion optimization will start with journey optimization. A team focused on editorial planning will start with high-intent content. A team focused on monetization deals will start with sponsor discovery. Each workflow has its own landing page with the relevant metrics, drill-downs, and reporting cadence.

### Caveats

Revenue signals are correlational, not causal. A page that ranks high on the high-intent-content view is the kind of page that, historically, attracted conversion-ready users — but conversion ultimately depends on factors outside the Platform (offer, pricing, payment UX, brand trust). Treat the Revenue section as a place to find leads worth investigating, not as a forecasting tool.
